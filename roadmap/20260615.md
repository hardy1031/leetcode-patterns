# FAANG Readiness Roadmap — Jun 15, 2026

Be ready before the fall sprint, not "as many as possible" — for FAANG new-grad/intern coding rounds, ~150 well-understood problems covering the core patterns is the real bar, not a 500-problem grind; the count is just a proxy for the thing that's actually tested.

---

## What's Actually Being Measured

- **Pattern recognition** — can you see "this is a sliding-window / BFS / DP problem" within ~2 min?
- **Speed** — can you solve a new medium in ~25–35 min with a near-optimal solution?
- **Communication** — can you talk through your reasoning out loud the whole time?

A person who deeply understands 150 problems beats someone who blind-grinded 400. So the target isn't a number — it's coverage of the ~15 core patterns + the three skills above.

---

## Pattern Checklist

Arrays/hashing · two pointers · sliding window · stack · binary search · linked list · trees (BFS/DFS) · tries · heaps/priority queue · backtracking · graphs (BFS/DFS/union-find) · 1-D DP · 2-D DP · intervals · greedy · bit manipulation

---

## Two Rules That Matter More Than Volume

1. **Redo > new.** Re-solving a problem you failed (after a few days) builds the pattern memory that actually transfers. Spaced repetition beats novelty.
2. **Struggle 20–30 min, then look.** Don't peek early (no learning) and don't grind 2 hours (waste).

---

## Readiness Test

Pick a random unseen LC medium, set a 35-min timer, solve it out loud. If you can do that ~80% of the time by late August, you're FAANG-ready — regardless of whether you've done 130 or 230.
