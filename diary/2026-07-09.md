## Theme: Min-Heap of Fixed Size k (Top-K Pattern)

First heap problem. To track the k largest elements efficiently, use a min-heap of size k — not a max-heap. The heap top is the smallest of the k largest, which is exactly the kth largest. When a new element arrives, push it and pop the top if size exceeds k.

### Key insight
A min-heap of size k always exposes the kth largest at the top in O(1). This is the canonical "top-k" pattern reused across many heap problems.
